Frequently Asked Questions

Questions
How does Precident-AI work?

Precident-AI uses machine learning models to analyze students' academic data. By inputting relevant data, the system predicts whether a student will pass or need to retake a course.

Questions
What do the prediction results mean?

The prediction results from Precident-
AI can be:
- Yes Repeat Paper: The student is predicted to repeat the course.
- No Repeat Paper: The student is predicted not to repeat the course.
- PASS: The student has passed the course.
- FAIL: The student has failed the course.

Questions
Are the predictions 100% accurate?

No. Precident-AI provides predictions based on available data and the machine learning model used. Although the model has been trained with relevant data, the final outcome still depends on the student's effort and other academic factors.

Questions
Can this platform be used for fields other than dentistry?

Currently, Precident-AI is focused on predicting the academic performance of dental students. However, future developments may expand its use to other fields as needed.

Questions
Who can use this platform?

This platform can be used by:
- Dental students to understand their academic potential.
- Lecturers and academic staff to identify students at risk of academic difficulties.
- Academic administration to design better learning strategies.

Questions
What should I do if I receive a prediction of 'Yes Repeat Paper' or 'FAIL'?

If you receive such predictions, it is recommended that you:
- Consult with your lecturer or academic advisor.
- Utilize additional resources such as tutoring or study groups.
- Improve study strategies to enhance understanding of course material.
